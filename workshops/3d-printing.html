3D Printing

Overview

A workshop centered on engineering decision-making and equipment operation: how design selections, material choices, and print orientation influence strength, surface quality, cost, and manufacturability.

Covered

  • Evaluating whether 3D printing or an alternative method fits a given prototype
  • The range of prototyping equipment available in the lab
  • How print orientation affects final results
  • Reducing support-material waste through smarter orientation

Notes

Emphasized fast prototyping and iterative design under practical constraints, including that "PLA printers aren't advised for force-dependent applications."
